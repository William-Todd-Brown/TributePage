Margaret Heafield Hamilton

Margaret Heafield Hamilton in 1969, standing next to listings of the software she and her MIT team produced for the Apollo project.

Here's a time line of Margaret Heafield Hamilton's life:

  • 1935 - Born in Paoli, Indiana
  • 1958 - She Studied Mathematics at the University of Michigan in 1955 than transferred to Earlham College and earned a BA in Mathematics with a minor in philosophy.
  • 1959 - Hamilton began working for Edward Norton Lorenz in the meterology department at MIT. where she developed software for predicting weather.
  • 1961 - Hamilton hired and trained Ellen Fetter as her replacement, to focus on another project.
  • 1961 - 1963 - Hamilton worked on the Semi-Automatic Ground Environment(SAGE) Project at the MIT Lincoln Lab, where she was one of the programmers who wrote software used by the U.S. Air Force to search for possibly unfriendly aircraft
  • 1969 - Help Land Apollo 11 with her team from MIT on the moon.
  • 1976 - Hamilton co-founded with Saydean Zeldin a company called Higher Order Software to further develop ideas about error prevention and fault tolerance emerging from the experience at MIT working on the Apollo program.
  • 1984 - Left Higher Order Software
  • 1986 - Founded Hamilton Technologies, Inc. in Cambridge, Massachusetts. The company was developed around the Universal Systems Language and its associated automated environment based on her paradigm of development before the fact for systems design and software development.
  • 2016 - She received the Presidential medal of Freedom from Barack Obama, the highest civilian honor in the United States
  • 2019 - She was awared an honoary doctorate degree by Bard College.
